This is the system I am most proud of having built. Follicle Labeller is a cross-platform desktop and web tool that helps clinicians annotate hair grafts on high-resolution scalp images and track them through live surgical video.

It replaced a licensed third-party annotation platform, eliminated recurring licensing costs, and runs on Windows, macOS (Intel and Apple Silicon), and the browser from a single codebase.

## Why we built it

The third-party tool we were using had three problems: it cost a lot per seat, it could not handle 8K imagery without crashing, and the export format was proprietary so we could not feed annotations directly into our training pipeline. Building our own took the licensing problem and turned it into an in-house asset.

## The hard parts

### Inference across four hardware backends

The same model has to run on:
- Clinician laptops (Apple Silicon, CoreML)
- Workstations in the clinic (NVIDIA CUDA)
- Older Intel MacBooks (CPU fallback)
- The browser (ONNX Runtime Web)

I picked YOLO11 specifically because it exports cleanly to all four. Maintaining four inference paths is the price of supporting a heterogeneous deployment.

### Real-time tracking

Detection alone is not enough. Surgeons need to see the same graft followed across frames of live video. I combined three techniques:
- Template matching for short-range continuity
- Multi-object tracking (DeepSORT) for medium-range association
- Optical flow when grafts leave and re-enter the field of view

Hit rate: 90%+ in production conditions. The remaining 10% require manual re-identification - which is a UI problem, not a model problem.

### One codebase, three platforms

Tauri for the desktop shell, Next.js for the web, shared TypeScript core. ONNX Runtime as the inference layer because it has bindings for all three targets. Auto-update on desktop, CI/CD to ship updates without clinician intervention.

## Numbers

- 5 supported image formats (PNG, JPG, TIFF, DICOM, plus a custom intermediate)
- 3 annotation types (bounding boxes, keypoints, polygons)
- 80%+ detection accuracy on 2K/4K/8K clinical imagery
- 90%+ tracking accuracy across surgical video
- 1 codebase, 4 inference backends, 3 deployment targets

## What I learned

Production AI is 10% modeling and 90% systems. The detection model is the smallest part of Follicle Labeller. The hard parts are the inference graph, the cross-platform shell, the annotation UX, the keyboard shortcuts that clinicians actually use under time pressure. That is where the work is.
